How This Works

  • Learn 1 million playlists & 2.2 million songs from Spotify
  • Create our own database
  • Train our model
  • Analyze your favorite songs
  • Predict your next favorite song

Key insights - 3 different ML methods to approach this problem

Model 1 - Cosine-Similarity

Is a method used to evaluate similarity of vectors by calculating the cosine of their angles. In our case, we compare an user's playlist vector to all the song vectors in our database. The features we use for formulating the vectors are audio features, such as loudness, energy, acoustics, instrumentalness, liveness, etc. We rank the similarity values of each track by a descending order and recommend the top ones to the user.

Model 2 - Tracks Clustering

We use K-means clustering to group the tracks into different clusters based on their audio features. The user's playlist is then compared to the clusters and the tracks in the most similar cluster are recommended to the user.

Model 3 - Playlists Clustering

We use K-means clustering to group the playlists into different clusters based on their audio features. The user's playlist is then compared to the clusters and the tracks in the most similar cluster are recommended to the user.
